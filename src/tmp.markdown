### Breakfast: Brioche French Toast Stuffed with Cream Cheese and Fresh Peaches

**Ingredients**

- 4 slices thick-cut Brioche bread
- 2 large eggs
- 100mL milk
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
- 100g cream cheese, softened
- 2 fresh peaches, pitted and diced
- Powdered sugar for dusting

**Steps**

1. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, and vanilla extract.
2. Over medium heat, melt the butter in a large skillet.
3. Dip each slice of brioche into the egg mixture, ensuring both sides are well coated, then place in the skillet.
4. Cook the brioche until golden brown, about 3 to 5 minutes per side.
5. While the brioche is cooking, mix the softened cream cheese and diced peaches in a separate bowl until combined.
6. Once the brioche is cooked, let cool briefly before cutting a pocket into each slice and filling with the cream cheese-peach mixture.
7. Dust the top of each slice with powdered sugar and serve immediately.

### Lunch: Turkey & Cheese Panini

**Ingredients**

- 4 slices sourdough bread
- 200g smoked turkey breast, sliced
- 4 slices provolone cheese
- 2 tablespoons sundried tomato spread (store-bought - Delallo Sun-Dried Tomato Bruschetta)
- Fresh basil leaves

**Steps**

1. Spread a layer of sundried tomato spread on one side of each slice of bread.
2. Layer the smoked turkey, provolone cheese, and fresh basil leaves on top of the spread on two of the slices.
3. Top with the other two slices of bread, spread-side down.
4. Heat a panini press to medium-high heat.
5. Place the sandwiches in the press, close the lid, and cook until the cheese is melted and the bread is toasted, about 4 to 5 minutes. Serve hot.

### Dinner: BBQ Chicken Drumsticks with Corn on the Cob and Coleslaw

**Ingredients**

**BBQ Chicken Drumsticks**
- 8 chicken drumsticks
- 250mL BBQ sauce (store-bought - Sweet Baby Ray's Honey BBQ Sauce)
- Salt and pepper to taste

**Corn on the Cob**
- 4 ears of corn
- 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
- Salt to taste

**Coleslaw**
- 500g coleslaw mix (store-bought)
- 100mL coleslaw dressing (store-bought - Marzetti Original Slaw Dressing)

**Steps**

1. Preheat your grill or barbecue to medium heat.
2. Season the chicken drumsticks with salt and pepper.
3. Brush the drumsticks with BBQ sauce and grill for about 25 to 30 minutes, or until cooked through, turning and basting with more sauce occasionally.
4. While the chicken is cooking, bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
5. Add the corn and boil for about 5 to 7 minutes, or until tender.
6. Remove the corn from the water, pat dry, and brush with butter.
7. Sprinkle the corn with a little salt before serving.
8. To prepare the coleslaw, simply mix the coleslaw mix with the dressing in a large bowl until well coated.
9. Serve the BBQ chicken drumsticks with a side of corn on the cob and coleslaw.
