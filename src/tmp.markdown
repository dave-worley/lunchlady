### Breakfast: Authentic Huevos Rancheros with Refried Beans, Roasted Tomato Salsa, and Handmade Corn Tortillas

#### Ingredients
- 8 large eggs
- 400g canned black beans, drained and rinsed
- 50g lard or vegetable oil
- 4g ground cumin
- Salt to taste
- 8 handmade corn tortillas (or store-bought)
- 100g queso fresco, crumbled
- 20g fresh cilantro leaves, chopped
- Roasted Tomato Salsa:
    - 500g ripe tomatoes
    - 1 onion, quartered
    - 2 jalapeño peppers
    - 10g minced garlic
    - 30ml olive oil
    - Salt and pepper to taste

#### Steps
1. For the salsa, preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). Place the tomatoes, onion, and jalapeños on a baking sheet. Drizzle with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
2. Roast the vegetables for 20-25 minutes or until lightly charred and softened.
3. Transfer the roasted vegetables to a blender, add the garlic, and blend until smooth. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
4. For the refried beans, heat the lard or oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the black beans and cumin. Mash the beans with a potato masher or the back of a spoon until smooth. Season with salt to taste.
5. In a separate skillet, fry the eggs to your desired doneness.
6. Warm the corn tortillas according to package instructions or on a skillet.
7. To assemble, spread a spoonful of refried beans on each tortilla. Top with a fried egg, a generous spoonful of roasted tomato salsa, crumbled queso fresco, and chopped cilantro.
8. Serve the huevos rancheros immediately.

### Dinner: Pressure Cooker Chicken Provençal with Olives and Tomatoes, Served with Ratatouille and Crusty Bread

#### Ingredients
- 1kg bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
- 4g salt
- 2g black pepper
- 30ml olive oil
- 1 onion, diced
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 200ml dry white wine
- 400g canned diced tomatoes
- 100g Kalamata olives, pitted
- 10g fresh thyme leaves
- 10g fresh basil leaves, chopped
- Ratatouille:
    - 1 eggplant, diced
    - 2 zucchini, diced
    - 1 red bell pepper, diced
    - 1 onion, diced
    - 20g minced garlic
    - 400g canned diced tomatoes
    - 30ml olive oil
    - 10g fresh basil leaves, chopped
    - Salt and pepper to taste
- 1 loaf crusty bread, sliced

#### Steps
1. Season the chicken thighs with salt and pepper.
2. Set the pressure cooker to the sauté function. Heat the olive oil and brown the chicken thighs on both sides, working in batches if necessary. Remove the chicken and set aside.
3. Add the onion to the pressure cooker and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Add the garlic and cook for an additional minute.
4. Pour in the white wine and deglaze the pot, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom.
5. Add the diced tomatoes, olives, thyme, and browned chicken thighs to the pressure cooker. Close and lock the lid.
6. Cook on high pressure for 20 minutes, then allow the pressure to release naturally for 10 minutes before manually releasing any remaining pressure.
7. While the chicken is cooking, prepare the ratatouille. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the eggplant, zucchini, bell pepper, onion, and garlic. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables are softened, about 10 minutes.
8. Stir in the diced tomatoes and simmer for an additional 10 minutes. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Stir in the fresh basil.
9. Remove the chicken from the pressure cooker and stir the fresh basil into the sauce.
10. Serve the chicken Provençal with the ratatouille and crusty bread on the side.
